2019-2020 Australian Bush Fires
(January 2020 Near Real Time Data)

The Australian bush fire had been devastating. It has caused
massive loss of wildlife, forest land, and has even to
led to human casualties. There have been huge undertakings by the
fire department on controlling the bush fires and rehabilation of the forests.

The NRT (Near Real Time) data was collected by MODIS
(Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer)

The Near Real Time (NRT) data is between
January 1st, 2020 to January 31th, 2020.

Total Near Real Time Bush Fire Spread In January 2020

In January 2020,
there was a total of 34857 records by the MODIS intrument.
Two satellites, Terra and Aqua, were used to record these readings.
The highest Fire Radiative Power is 11164.1 units.
The average confidence of the accuracy recorded is 75.38%.

Amount Of Fire Per Day In January 2020

Select either Day Time (D) or Night Time (N) in the Legend to view respective data.

There is a total of 20394 readings recorded during daytime.
There is a total of 14462 readings recorded during nighttime.
There is a huge increase in readings recorded on the
4th January 2020 compared to 3rd January 2020.
(Increase from 4257 records to 7350 records)

Interactive Map

Select the first slider to filter the Fire Radiative Power level.
Select the second slider to zoom in/out of the map.
Select the dropdown menu to select areas that have the most fires.

The areas with the most fire spread in January 2020 are:
Kangaroo Island, South Australia
Abbeyard, Victoria
Cobberas, Victoria
Canberra, New South Wales
Kalgoorlie, West Australia

Analysis of the Australia Bush Fires

Dates on which bushfires were at a peak in Australia:
1. 4th January 2020.
2. 30th December 2019.
3. 8th January 2020.

Regions with highest recorded fire radiation power in a day:
1. Abbeyard, Victoria on the 8th January 2020.

3. Ravine Road, Flinders Chase in Kangaroo Island

5. West Bay Road, Flinders Chase in Kangaroo Island on the 8th January 2020.
